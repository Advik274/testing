# ⚡ DOMINANCE — PARTICIPANT GUIDE

Welcome to **Dominance** — a 3-phase tech battle where only the sharpest teams survive.

---

## THE BASICS

You're part of a team. Your goal: **outlast, outsmart, and outbuild** every other team.

There are 3 phases. Lose in Phase 1, you sit out. Lose your battery in Phase 2, you're out. Make it to Phase 3, and you build for glory.

---

## YOUR TEAM DASHBOARD

Every team gets:
- A **4-digit passcode** (guard it — it's how you log in everywhere)
- A **team color** and name
- A **battery meter** (Phase 2) — don't let it hit 0%

Open your team dashboard on any phone or laptop. Ask your coordinator for the URL.

---

## PHASE 1 — THE GAUNTLET
> *~30 minutes | All teams compete simultaneously*

### What happens:
- Questions appear on your screen — MCQ, logic puzzles, output prediction
- You have 20–45 seconds per question
- Tap the correct answer as fast as possible — **speed matters, it affects your score**
- After each round, the lowest-scoring teams are **eliminated**

### Tips:
- Discuss quickly — you have seconds, not minutes
- Wrong answer = 0. No answer = 0. Take a shot if you're unsure.
- Faster correct answers outscore slower correct answers

### You're eliminated if:
- You're in the bottom teams after a round ends

---

## PHASE 2 — ZONE WARS
> *90 minutes | Physical movement around campus*

This is the main event. Teams move around campus capturing **physical zones**.

### Your Battery:
You start with **100% battery**. It drains over time.

| Action | Battery |
|--------|---------|
| Attempt a zone challenge | **−5%** |
| Capture a zone | **+20%** |
| Every minute | −2% (auto drain) |
| Battery = 0% | **Eliminated** |

### How to Capture a Zone:
1. **Walk to a zone** (your coordinator will give you a map)
2. Tell the zone operator your **team passcode**
3. Wait — the system loads your challenge (or picks one if it's neutral)
4. **Read the problem carefully** — you have **5 minutes**
5. **Type your answer** directly on the zone laptop
6. Hit Submit

### Types of Challenges:
- **Debugging** — find and fix bugs in code
- **Output Trace** — predict what code prints
- **Logic Puzzle** — solve a multi-step problem
- **SQL/Query** — database reasoning
- **Cipher/Encoding** — decode a message

All challenges are solvable in under 5 minutes for a team of 3–5 people. **Work together.**

### Captured Zones:
- Once you capture a zone, **your team must set a trap** for the next team
- Go to your team dashboard → you'll see a prompt to pick 1 of 5 problems
- **Pick wisely** — harder traps slow down attackers

### Zone Rules:
- ❌ You cannot recapture your own zone
- ❌ Only one team can attempt a zone at a time — if it shows busy, come back
- ✅ You can attempt the same zone again after another team has captured it
- ✅ Capturing zones earns battery — it's worth the risk

### Strategy Tips:
- Prioritize zones that are currently neutral (no defender) — no trap to solve
- Watch the leaderboard — chase zones held by low-battery teams
- Don't waste battery on zones where you're unsure — failed attempts cost 5%
- Set hard traps on your zones to slow rivals

---

## PHASE 3 — BUILD SPRINT
> *2.5 hours | Surviving teams build a web app*

If you survived Phases 1 and 2 — congratulations. Now build something real.

### What happens:
1. A **problem statement** is revealed — you'll build a web app around it
2. You have **150 minutes** to build and deploy
3. Submit before time runs out via your team dashboard:
   - **Deployment URL** — your live, working app (Vercel, Netlify, etc.)
   - **GitHub Repository URL** — your code
   - Notes to judges (optional but helpful)

### Judging Criteria:

| Criterion | Weight | What they look for |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| 🎨 UI Design | 30% | Clean, usable, looks intentional |
| ⚙️ Functionality | 40% | Core features actually work |
| 🎯 Problem Fit | 30% | Does it solve the stated problem? |

**Total: /100**

### Build Tips:
- Start with the core feature — get it working first, style later
- Use frameworks you already know (React, plain HTML, anything)
- Deploy early — Vercel/Netlify take 2 minutes. Don't scramble at the end.
- The notes field is your chance to explain what you built and what you'd add

---

## RESULTS REVEAL

After judging, results are announced **live on all screens** — worst to best.

Each team gets a dramatic full-screen reveal. The winner gets 🏆 confetti.
